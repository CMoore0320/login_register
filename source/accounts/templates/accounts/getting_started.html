Welcome to The House Brain!

Thank you for joining us. Below are some steps to help you get started with using our platform:

Getting Started Guide

  1. Add an Address: Using the "Add Address" tab, save addresses of the properties you would like to track.
  2. Add Equipment: Once you have an address, add equipment to track using the "Add Equipment" tab.
  3. Add Maintenance Records: Once you have equipment added, you can start tracking maintenance completed for that equipment using the "Add Maintenance" tab.
  4. Track and Save Receipts: Receipt images and information from the receipt, to include price and date, can be tracked using the "Add Receipt" tab.
  5. Start Being Organized: Begin tracking maintenance and having information available in one convenient place.
  6. Reports, Records and Receipts: Will provide useful information about your house!

Tips for Maintenance Tracking

Here are some tips for effectively tracking maintenance items:

  • Regular Checkups: Schedule routine inspections to identify potential issues early.
  • Record Details: Keep detailed records of maintenance activities, including dates, costs, and any notes.
  • Check the Dashboard: Use our platform as a reminder for upcoming maintenance tasks.
  • Review Reports: Periodically review reports to assess the overall status of your maintenance efforts.

Common Maintenance Items to Track

Here are some common maintenance items and their recommended frequencies:

  • Change HVAC Filters: Every 1-3 months, depending on filter type and usage.
  • Change Water Filters: Every 6 months, depending on manufacturer recommendations.
  • Clean Gutters: Twice a year, in spring and fall, or more frequently if necessary.
  • Inspect Roof: Annually, or after severe weather events.
  • Test Smoke Detectors: Monthly, to ensure they are functioning properly.
  • Flush Water Heater: Every 6-12 months, to remove sediment buildup.
  • Check Plumbing for Leaks: Regularly inspect pipes and faucets for leaks.
  • Service Lawn Equipment: Annually, before the start of the mowing season.
  • Inspect Fire Extinguishers: Annually, to verify they are fully charged and functional.
  • Clean Dryer Vents: Annually, to prevent fire hazards.
  • Inspect Electrical Wiring: Every 5-10 years, or if you notice any issues.
  • Clean Fire Place: Annually if used often, to prevent fire hazards.
